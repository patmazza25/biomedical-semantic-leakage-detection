# 1. Drug mechanism vs. outcome
Explain how statins work in the body, and describe how they prevent heart attacks.

# 2. Disease vs. symptom
Describe what asthma is and explain what symptoms people with asthma typically experience.

# 3. General vs. specific mechanism
What are antibiotics and how does penicillin in particular work against bacteria?

# 4. Mechanism vs. limitation
How does chemotherapy attack cancer cells, and why does it also harm healthy cells?

# 5. Preventive vs. curative framing
Explain how vaccines help with influenza, and whether they can cure someone who already has the flu.

# 6. Different body systems
What does insulin do in regulating blood sugar, and does it have any effect on blood pressure?

# 7. Drug vs. lifestyle intervention
Explain how exercise helps the heart, and how beta-blockers reduce cardiac workload.

# 8. Orthogonal drug effects
What are the main effects of aspirin in the body, both for fever and for blood clot prevention?

# 9. Symptom vs. disease cause
Explain how ibuprofen helps when someone has an infection, and whether it can cure the infection itself.

# 10. Risk vs. mechanism
Why does diabetes increase cardiovascular disease risk, and how does high blood sugar damage blood vessels?
